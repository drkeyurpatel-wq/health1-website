ACL Tear: Symptoms, Surgery, and Realistic Recovery Timeline

Medically reviewed by Dr. Rohan Vakta, Sports Medicine Specialist · Updated July 7, 2026
An ACL tear typically causes a "pop" sensation at the moment of injury, followed by swelling within hours and a feeling of knee instability. Most complete tears in active or athletic individuals require arthroscopic reconstruction surgery, with return to sport realistically taking 6-9 months.

Recognizing an ACL Tear

  • A "pop" sound or sensation at the moment of injury (common but not universal)
  • Rapid swelling within a few hours
  • A feeling that the knee is unstable or "gives way"
  • Pain with weight-bearing, though some can still walk shortly after injury
  • Loss of full range of motion

Do All ACL Tears Need Surgery?

Not necessarily. Partial tears, or complete tears in less active individuals who don't plan to return to pivoting sports, can sometimes be managed with physiotherapy and bracing. However, most complete tears in athletes or physically active people are reconstructed surgically, because the ACL has limited ability to heal on its own and knee instability increases the risk of further cartilage or meniscus damage over time.

The Surgery Itself

ACL reconstruction is done arthroscopically (through small incisions) using a graft — often taken from your own hamstring or patellar tendon — to rebuild the ligament. It's typically same-day or overnight surgery, not a major open procedure.

Realistic Recovery Timeline

  • Weeks 1-2: Swelling control, gentle range-of-motion exercises, walking with crutches.
  • Weeks 3-6: Progressive strengthening, often walking without crutches by week 4-6.
  • Months 2-4: Running and agility drills begin, under physiotherapist guidance.
  • Months 6-9: Sport-specific training and gradual return to competitive play, based on strength testing — not just the calendar.

The 6-9 month window isn't arbitrary — it reflects how long the graft takes to biologically mature and integrate. Returning to pivoting sports before the graft has matured meaningfully increases re-injury risk, even if the knee feels fine.

Common Questions

Can I walk on a torn ACL?

Some people can walk immediately after an ACL tear, especially if other structures aren't also damaged, but the knee often feels unstable — this doesn't mean the injury isn't serious, and it should still be evaluated promptly.

What happens if an ACL tear goes untreated?

Ongoing instability increases the risk of secondary injuries to the meniscus and cartilage over time, which is why even non-surgical management involves structured physiotherapy to protect the joint.

Is ACL surgery painful?

Post-surgical pain is managed with medication and is typically most significant in the first few days, improving steadily; most patients describe the rehabilitation process as more demanding than the pain itself.
