When Reconstructive Surgery Is Considered

Significant burns needing specialized wound care
Traumatic injury affecting function or appearance
Tissue loss after cancer surgery
Non-healing wounds
Congenital defects affecting function

How Reconstructive Care Is Planned

Reconstruction is planned around restoring function first, appearance second — though both matter. For burns and major trauma, this often means staged procedures over weeks to months, starting with wound stabilization and progressing through grafting or flap reconstruction as healing allows. Your surgeon will walk through a realistic staged timeline specific to your injury.

Common Questions

Is plastic surgery only cosmetic?

No — reconstructive plastic surgery restores function and form after burns, trauma, cancer surgery, or congenital conditions, and is a distinct focus from purely cosmetic procedures.

How many surgeries does major burn reconstruction typically need?

This varies widely by burn severity and location — from a single procedure to a staged series over months; your surgeon will outline an expected plan after initial assessment.

Is hand surgery part of this speciality?

Yes — hand trauma and reconstruction, including nerve and tendon repair, falls under plastic and reconstructive surgery.
