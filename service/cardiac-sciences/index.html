A 24×7 Cathlab, Not a 9-to-5 One

Heart attacks don't keep office hours. Health1's cardiac catheterization laboratory at Shilaj operates round the clock, so a patient arriving at 3 AM gets the same door-to-balloon pathway as one arriving at 3 PM. Emergency angiography identifies the blocked artery; primary angioplasty opens it — often within the golden hour that protects heart muscle.

Beyond emergencies, the same Cathlab supports planned angiography, elective angioplasty with stenting, pacemaker and device implantation, and structural interventions such as device closures for septal defects.

When Chest Pain Means Come Now

Crushing or squeezing chest pain, pain spreading to the left arm, jaw or back, breathlessness, cold sweats, or sudden collapse — treat these as a heart attack until proven otherwise. Do not drive yourself; call our 24×7 emergency line 90331 14444 and reach the nearest Health1 emergency department. Every Health1 centre can stabilize a cardiac patient and fast-track transfer to the Cathlab when needed.

Common Questions

How quickly can angioplasty be done in an emergency?

Our Cathlab at Shilaj runs 24×7 with an on-call interventional cardiology team. Once a heart attack is confirmed on ECG, the patient moves directly to the Cathlab — the aim is to open the blocked artery as fast as possible, because time saved is heart muscle saved.

What is the difference between angiography and angioplasty?

Angiography is the diagnostic test — dye is injected through a catheter to see blockages in the heart arteries. Angioplasty is the treatment — a balloon opens the blocked artery and usually a stent is placed to keep it open. Both are done through a small puncture in the wrist or groin, not open surgery.

Is cardiac treatment at Health1 cashless?

Yes, for insured patients — Health1 is empanelled with major insurers, TPAs, CGHS and PMJAY. In emergencies, treatment starts immediately and our insurance desk processes the cashless approval in parallel.

Do I need a referral to see a cardiologist?

No. You can book a consultation directly through this website, by calling 079 6999 6999, or on WhatsApp. For chest pain or suspected heart attack, skip the appointment and come straight to Emergency.
