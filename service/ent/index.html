Routine Care, Advanced Backup

Most ENT visits are for common, treatable problems — a sinus infection, recurring tonsillitis, seasonal nasal allergy. Health1's team also includes fellowship-trained expertise in endoscopic sinus surgery and skull base surgery, so if a routine case turns out to be more complex, the same team can manage it rather than referring out.

When to See an ENT Specialist

Sinus symptoms lasting 12+ weeks
Recurring infections, 4+ times a year
Sudden or worsening hearing loss
Persistent hoarseness beyond 2-3 weeks
Vertigo affecting daily activity
A neck lump that doesn't resolve

What an ENT Evaluation Involves

Most visits start with a nasal endoscopy — a thin, flexible scope that lets the doctor see directly into the sinuses and throat in the clinic, without sedation. Hearing concerns are assessed with audiometry. Imaging (CT of the sinuses, or MRI for suspected tumors) is reserved for cases where the clinical exam points to something beyond routine infection or where surgery is being planned.

Common Questions

When does chronic sinusitis need surgery?

Surgery is considered when symptoms persist despite medication (antibiotics, nasal sprays) over several months, or when structural issues like polyps are blocking normal drainage.

Do all children with recurring tonsillitis need tonsils removed?

Not always — frequency and severity of infections, along with impact on breathing or sleep, guide this decision. Your ENT specialist will assess whether removal is warranted.

What causes sudden hearing loss, and is it an emergency?

Sudden hearing loss (over hours to a few days) can have several causes, some of which respond better to prompt treatment — it's worth being seen quickly rather than waiting to see if it resolves.
