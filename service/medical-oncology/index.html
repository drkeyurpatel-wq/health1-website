How Treatment Planning Works

A new diagnosis typically starts with a multidisciplinary review — oncology, radiology, and where relevant, surgery — to agree on a staged treatment plan rather than a single specialist deciding in isolation. Chemotherapy and targeted therapy schedules are built around your specific cancer type and stage, with regular scans to track response and adjust the plan as needed.

Common Questions

Is chemotherapy always required after surgery?

Not always — this depends on cancer type, stage, and risk of recurrence; your oncology team will explain the specific reasoning for your case.

Can I get a second opinion on my cancer treatment plan?

Yes — this is a common and reasonable request, especially before starting a major treatment course. See our Second Opinion service.

What is palliative care, and is it only for end-of-life situations?

Palliative care focuses on symptom relief and quality of life, and can be provided alongside active cancer treatment at any stage, not only at the end of life.
