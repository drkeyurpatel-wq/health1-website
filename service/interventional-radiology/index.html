What an IR Procedure Involves

Most interventional radiology procedures are done through a small needle puncture or catheter, guided in real time by imaging, under local anaesthesia or light sedation — not general anaesthesia. This typically means same-day or overnight recovery, versus the longer recovery of equivalent open surgery.

Common Questions

Is interventional radiology surgery?

It's a distinct, less invasive alternative to surgery for many conditions — performed through a needle or catheter rather than an open incision, though it does involve real procedural risk and recovery like any medical intervention.

How is a fibroid embolization different from a hysterectomy?

Embolization blocks blood supply to fibroids through a catheter, shrinking them while preserving the uterus — a fundamentally different, less invasive approach than surgical removal.

Is sedation used for these procedures?

Most use local anaesthesia with light sedation rather than general anaesthesia, though this varies by procedure — your IR specialist will confirm what's planned for your specific case.
