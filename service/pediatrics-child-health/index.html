When to Bring Your Child In

Fever lasting more than 2-3 days
Feeding difficulty in infants
Delayed developmental milestones
Persistent fatigue or pallor
Unexplained bruising or bleeding

What a Paediatric Visit Involves

Routine visits track growth against standard milestones — weight, height, and developmental markers — alongside age-appropriate vaccination. For more specialized concerns, like suspected blood disorders, our paediatric hematology expertise means complex cases can be managed without referring the family to a separate facility.

Common Questions

What vaccinations are recommended and when?

This follows a standard schedule from birth through adolescence — your paediatrician will provide the specific timeline and confirm which vaccines your child is due for at each visit.

When is a developmental delay actually concerning?

Occasional variation in milestone timing is normal, but consistent delay across multiple areas (motor, speech, social) warrants evaluation rather than a wait-and-see approach.

What does a paediatric hematologist treat?

Blood-related conditions in children — anemia, clotting disorders, and some blood cancers — that need more specialized management than general paediatric care.
